# Phase 2 Launch Positioning Sheet

## Purpose

This file provides a single-page internal reference for the initial Phase 2 launch position.

It pulls together the key decisions already made so that service design, messaging, and launch materials stay anchored to the same buyer, problem, trigger, and first offer.

This file is living documentation.

---

## Priority

- **Priority:** P1
- **Work Type:** Service Design
- **Output Type:** Positioning Asset
- **Launch Critical:** Yes

---

## Definition of Done

This item is functioning as intended when the following are clear in one place:

- first buyer segment
- first offer
- problem language
- likely buying trigger
- primary positioning statement

---

## First Buyer Segment

**Founder-led marketing, creative, and digital agencies that have real delivery capability but need clearer service structure, sharper problem/solution language, and more usable internal documentation to support growth.**

### Why this buyer first

- the pain is common and recognizable
- the value is understandable without heavy education
- the problem is close to growth, delegation, and delivery consistency
- the need aligns directly to the current service design direction
- the work can be described in practical business terms

---

## First Offer

**Agency Service Clarity Sprint**

### Offer Summary

A focused service for founder-led agencies that need clearer service language, better internal structure, and more usable documentation to support growth, delegation, and delivery consistency.

---

## Core Problem

The agency does good work, but too much of the service model still depends on founder knowledge, informal process, and scattered documentation.

### Plain-Language Version

You have a real service business, but too much of it still lives in your head, your habits, or scattered documents.

---

## Current State

The business has likely grown through real client delivery rather than formal system design. As a result, parts of the service structure may be hard to explain, hard to repeat, and hard to delegate. Important knowledge may still sit with the founder instead of in usable structure.

---

## Likely Buying Trigger

**Growth, delegation, or delivery strain exposes the need for clearer service language, better internal structure, and more reusable documentation.**

### Common Trigger Moments

- the founder becomes the bottleneck
- onboarding support is harder than expected
- delivery feels too custom every time
- offer language no longer matches the real work
- the business is preparing to grow, stabilize, or delegate

---

## Core Solution

The service turns scattered service knowledge into clearer offers, better internal structure, and more usable documentation.

---

## Core Result

The business becomes easier to communicate, delegate, and run.

---

## Primary Positioning Statement

**Agency Service Clarity Sprint helps founder-led marketing, creative, and digital agencies turn scattered service knowledge into clearer offers, better internal structure, and usable documentation so the business becomes easier to communicate, delegate, and run.**

---

## One-Line Version

I help founder-led agencies make their services easier to explain, delegate, and run.

---

## Fit Signals

This is a strong fit when:

- the agency already has real client delivery
- the founder is still the main interpreter of how the business works
- service language feels broad, fuzzy, or outdated
- handoffs and delegation are harder than they should be
- the business needs more repeatability without a full operational overhaul

---

## Non-Fit Signals

This is a weaker fit when:

- the business is still only at idea stage
- the need is mainly branding, design execution, or campaign execution
- the buyer wants a fully built operations system immediately
- the main problem is not related to service clarity, workflow, or structure

---

## Typical Outputs

The first offer may produce:

- refined service summary
- target audience definition
- clarified problem/solution language
- core positioning statement
- simple workflow draft
- key documentation or structure gaps
- recommended next-step priorities

---

## Positioning Guidance

Frame this work as:

- clarity and structure support
- practical service enablement
- a way to make the agency easier to explain, delegate, and run

Do not frame this work primarily as:

- generic documentation help
- abstract consulting
- a full-scale agency operations overhaul
